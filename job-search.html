Job Search Intelligence

Application tracking. One coordinated system.

I coordinated my job search like a product launch — building a system to track target companies, monitor the pipeline, automate daily role scans, and measure conversion at every stage.

← All projects

The Problem

"I submitted applications with no system behind them. No visibility into which companies I was targeting, when I'd applied, who I'd followed up with, or what my conversion rate was. I was flying blind."

The Real Insight

A job search is a sales funnel. You need a CRM, not a spreadsheet. The moment I stopped managing it as a task list and started managing it as a pipeline — applications became top-of-funnel, not the goal. Conversion rate became the metric that mattered.

Decisions I Made and Why

Target Companies, Not Unlimited

Focused targeting beats shotgun applications. I built a curated list of companies across PropTech, AI, Fintech, and Enterprise SaaS — every one chosen because my background gave me a genuine edge. Started with 15 priority targets, expanded to 31 across tiers. Depth over breadth changes the entire dynamic.

Conversion Metrics, Not Application Count

Most job seekers optimise for applications sent. I built the system around conversion rates at each stage: applied → screen → interview → offer. That single decision changes what you optimise for entirely — and surfaced where the real drop-off was happening.

Daily Role Scans, Not Weekly Check-ins

Roles get posted and filled in 48–72 hours. An automated daily scan across all 31 target companies meant I could apply on day 1, before the volume of applicants made it impossible to stand out. First-mover advantage is real in job searching.

2-Week Follow-up Cadence, Not Ad Hoc

Systematic follow-up is the single highest-ROI action in a job search. I built automated reminders at 2-week intervals so no application ever went silent without intentional follow-up. Structured cadence reduces decision fatigue and creates consistent, deliberate touchpoints with hiring teams — not sporadic check-ins.

What the Data Told Me

A 10.5% screen rate is within industry average (8–12%) — but the standout signal is 100% screen-to-interview conversion rate: every company that scheduled a screen progressed to formal interviews. The top of the funnel is the current focus — increasing screen rate through tighter role targeting and resume version matching. The system is working; volume needs to increase.
